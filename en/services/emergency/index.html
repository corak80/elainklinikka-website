Emergency Veterinarian in Vaasa

Urgent care weekdays 9–17

Saari Animal Clinic is open Monday to Friday, 9–17. In acute situations we always try to make room for urgent cases the same day — call (06) 321 7300 first so we can assess the situation, give first-aid guidance for the journey, and prepare for your arrival. Arriving unannounced may lead to a long wait if scheduled patients are with us at the same time. The Vaasa region has no separate after-hours veterinary emergency service, but during weekday hours, urgent care is one phone call away.

When to call immediately

The following symptoms always warrant contacting a veterinarian without delay: breathing difficulty (open-mouth panting, blue gums, mouth-breathing in cats), unconsciousness or seizures, heavy bleeding or a large wound, traffic accident, fall, or other serious trauma, a cat unable to urinate (urinary blockage is life-threatening within hours), a dog with a swollen abdomen attempting to vomit unsuccessfully (gastric torsion is life-threatening), ingestion of toxic substances (chocolate, xylitol, antifreeze, grapes, onion, human medications), adder bite (Finland's only venomous snake — always requires veterinary assessment), severe vomiting or diarrhoea lasting over 24 hours, eye injury or sudden blindness, uncontrolled pain, a pregnant female in labour for over 30 minutes without progress.

What to do in an emergency

1. Call first: (06) 321 7300. We can prepare and give first-aid advice for the journey. 2. Keep your pet warm and calm. When transporting trauma patients, use a flat surface — a blanket or shallow box — lifting in your arms can worsen spinal or joint injuries. 3. With suspected poisoning, bring the packaging or a sample of any vomit. Do not attempt to induce vomiting at home without consulting us first — in some poisonings (corrosive substances, sharp objects) it is dangerous. 4. Do not give human medications. Paracetamol is fatal to cats, ibuprofen damages a dog's gastrointestinal tract and kidneys. 5. Recognise shock: cold ears and paws, pale gums, rapid shallow breathing, lethargy — shock requires immediate fluid therapy.

Evenings and weekends

Saari Animal Clinic is not a 24/7 emergency facility, and the Vaasa region has no after-hours small-animal emergency service. In the evenings (after 17:00) and on weekends, the nearest 24/7 emergency clinics are in Tampere and Helsinki, approximately 3–4 hours' drive away. For life-threatening situations we recommend calling your chosen 24/7 clinic first to confirm they can take you and to receive first-aid guidance for the long journey. You can also call us — even when closed, we check voicemail first thing in the morning and arrange the first available appointment when needed.

Most common emergencies and background

We have collected detailed information about some of the most common emergencies on our site. Read more as needed: adder bite — treatment and first aid, pyometra (uterine infection) and acute diarrhoea in dogs and cats. Recognising the situation early significantly improves treatment outcomes.
